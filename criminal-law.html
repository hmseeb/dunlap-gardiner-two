Asset Forfeiture

Civil & Criminal Asset Forfeiture Defense

Asset forfeiture is one of the most aggressive powers the government can exercise — and it requires swift, experienced legal response.

🏛 What is Asset Forfeiture?

Asset forfeiture occurs when the government seizes property it claims is connected to criminal activity. This can happen even before a conviction — or even if you are never charged with a crime.

There has been a major increase in State and Federal governments exercising their power of forfeiture using complicated technical statutes that often require swift responses to protect your rights.

⚠ Time-Sensitive Action Required

In forfeiture cases, deadlines can be extremely short. Missing a deadline may permanently forfeit your right to contest the seizure — even if you are ultimately found innocent of any crime.

If your assets have been seized, contact Dunlap Gardiner immediately at 770-489-5122.

👥 Who We Represent

We represent individuals whose assets have been seized due to criminal charges or convictions, as well as innocent third parties — such as lienholders — who may have a legitimate claim to seized property.

⚖ Civil vs. Criminal Forfeiture

Civil forfeiture allows the government to seize property without a criminal conviction. Criminal forfeiture is tied to a criminal conviction. Both require experienced legal defense, and the standards differ significantly.
